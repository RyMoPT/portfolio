This virtual goniometer is an easy to use tool for clinicians to measure angles of joint range of motion during live virtual care sessions. Built with Javascript and available as a Chrome extension in the Chrome Web Store.

Based on an original game from the Roz and Mocha show at Kiss 92.5. Try to give 5 answers in 7 seconds! Made with Javascript and Web Speech API. Currently only supported on PC and the latest Chrome browser due to Web Speech API limitations.







About


I always liked creating things, technology, and helping others.

I decided to become a healthcare practitioner because that way I could help others and make a difference in people's lives.

I ended up dedicating my post-secondary education to pursuing a career in physiotherapy.

After becoming a physiotherapist, I realized I still had that need to create and develop things. I also realized that I could make a difference in people's lives indirectly through technology. In fact, I could probably impact more people indirectly through technology than I ever could face to face over a lifetime.

One day I thought I would give programming a try.

Through resources such as The Odin Project, MDN, and YouTube, I started learning everything I needed to develop the things I wanted to create.

Being self taught has allowed me to learn effectively, efficiently, and quickly. I am constantly learning new concepts, tools, and languages in order to develop whatever ideas come to mind.
