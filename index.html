PTCR

Physiotherapy Course Reviews (PTCR) was developed to form a community of physiotherapists that leave honest and critical reviews of post graduate courses. The reason being is that there are a vast amount of courses available, and they cost anywhere from a few hundred to tens of thousands of dollars. I want clinicians to have the opportunity to determine if a course is right for them before signing up. I used this as an opportunity to become more familiar with React as well as backend tools such as Node and Express. I also used it as a chance to learn SQL with PostgreSQL.



Virtual Goniometer

This virtual goniometer is an easy to use tool for clinicians to measure angles of joint range of motion during live virtual care sessions. When the pandemic hit and most healthcare visits became virtual, I noticed there was a lack in obtaining objective measures. As a clinician, getting range of motion to monitor patient progress is critical. I saw this as an opportunity to use Javascript to approach this problem. Built with Javascript and available as a Chrome extension in the Chrome Web Store.



Weather App

Web application for determining current weather at any major city in the world. The goal of this project was to get familiar with a JS framework and APIs. It was developed with React, OpenWeather API, and Algolia Places API.



5 in 7

Based on an original game from the Roz and Mocha show at Kiss 92.5. Try to give 5 answers in 7 seconds! I decided to make a very basic version of this very fun but difficult game just to see if I could do it. Made with Javascript and Web Speech API. Currently only supported on PC and the latest Chrome browser due to Web Speech API limitations.







About


I always liked creating things, technology, and helping others.

I decided to become a healthcare practitioner so that I could help others and make a difference in people's lives.

I ended up dedicating my post-secondary education to pursuing a career in physiotherapy.

After becoming a physiotherapist, I realized I still had that need to create and develop things. I also realized that I could make a difference in people's lives indirectly through technology, impacting more people than I could ever see face to face over a lifetime.

Through resources such as The Odin Project, MDN, and YouTube, I started learning everything I needed to develop the things I wanted to create.

Being self taught has allowed me to learn effectively, efficiently, and quickly. I am constantly learning new concepts, tools, and languages in order to develop whatever ideas come to mind.
